Geography of Turkey

Explore the mountains, plateaus, fertile valleys, and coastal areas that shape this unique country.

Turkey is a vast country that bridges two continents: Europe and Asia. Covering an area of approximately 783,356 km², it is one of the largest in the region.

The country has a diverse geography, ranging from the rugged Taurus and Pontic mountain ranges, to fertile valleys and vast plateaus. The long coastline along the Mediterranean, Aegean, and Black Sea also creates unique climates and landscapes.

This geographical diversity makes Turkey a fascinating destination for travelers who enjoy exploring varied natural environments in one single trip.
